PROJECT DETAIL

The project initiated with a fundamental exploration of loan data, aiming to analyze various parameters and unveil crucial insights. Central to this endeavor was the meticulous examination of loan performance to determine risk segments, particularly focusing on charge-offs.

The primary objective encompassed the development of a comprehensive dashboard view, presenting an informative and intuitive summary of loan performance. This dynamic dashboard provides stakeholders with an easily digestible overview, empowering them to navigate and comprehend the loan data effectively, ultimately aiding in the formulation of strategic decisions for optimized risk management.

Goal

Identify Risk Factors: Analyze loan data to identify factors contributing to the risk associated with loans, particularly focusing on charge-offs.

Recommend Risk Mitigation Strategies: Propose strategies to manage risk, including lenient approaches for 36-month loans and stricter measures for 60-month loans.

Key Overview

The data analysis revealed that loans with a term of 60 months posed a higher risk compared to 36-month loans. Additionally, loans with an initial listing status fractional were also found to be riskier.

Conclusion

Based on these insights, the project recommended implementing lenient risk management strategies for 36-month loans and stricter measures for 60-month loans. This approach aims to optimize risk control and enhance loan portfolio performance.

Conclusion

In summary, our analysis of loan data revealed crucial insights. Loans spanning 60 months are noticeably riskier. Additionally, loans initially listed as fractional and not whole at the start carry higher risk. To mitigate this, we recommend more cautious approaches for 60-month loans and relaxed strategies for 36-month ones.

In essence, our project provides a practical foundation for thorough loan risk analysis. It's a stepping stone for further research and smarter risk management, aligning with real-world financial scenarios. The proposed dashboard is an instrumental tool for optimizing risk strategies and fostering better outcomes.
